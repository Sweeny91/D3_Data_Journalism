Correlations between Lack of Healthcare & Poverate Rates in the U.S.

The scatter plot shown above was constructed using US Census Bureau data in order to provide a visualization of a variety of variable comparisons.

The variables chosen for this particular scatter plot are the Poverty Rate vs the Lack of Healthcare in the United States. The variables were aggregated by state and the states abbreviations can be found on the center of the circles.

This plot insists that there is a strong positive correlation between the two variables. It can de suggested that as the rate on poverty increases, so does the lack of healthcare. The state with the worst healthcare retention rate is shown to be Texas while the state with the highest rate of poverty is Mississippi.
